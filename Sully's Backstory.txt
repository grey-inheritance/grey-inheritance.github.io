Solomon, or "Sully" as most know him, arrived in Barovia as a young man alongside his relatives and loved ones. They had traveled a great distance in search of somewhere to establish themselves as renowned tailors. The journey proved to be quite cruel. Many who had set out with him never reached their destination, and those who survived soon discovered an even harsher reality. Once inside Barovia, there was no leaving. Determined to make the best of their less than desirable circumstances, Sully and the few remaining members of his family settled in Vallaki. Life was difficult, but it was there that Sully found something worth cherishing. He met a kind-hearted woman named Edith and, before long, the two were married.


Their happiness was short-lived. Edith fell ill just a few years into their marriage and the medicine required to cure her could only be found beyond Barovia's borders. Trapped within the mists, Sully could do nothing but watch as hope slowly slipped through his fingers. He opened a small tailor's shop and worked tirelessly to afford the remedies available within Barovia. The medicines found locally could never cure her. They simply bought her time. The illness robbed Edith of many things, including the family she had always dreamed of having. Sully couldn’t bear seeing her spirits fade and did everything he could think to bring joy into her life.


One cold evening, shortly after Edith became ill, Sully found a small white kitten wandering the streets of Vallaki. The poor creature was injured and alone. He brought it home hoping it might bring Edith some comfort but it did far more than that. Edith adored the kitten from the moment she saw it. She affectionately named it "Boo" after its pure white fur and ghostly appearance. Over the years Boo grew into a remarkably clever and mischievous cat. Boo became Edith's constant companion and much more than just a pet. Whenever Edith’s condition worsened and she could no longer stand or walk, Boo would somehow sense it and rush to fetch Sully from his workshop.


For years the three of them formed a small family. Eventually, however, Edith's illness claimed her life. Her passing shattered Sully. For years his days had revolved around caring for her. Sully no longer knew who he was or what purpose remained for him. He continued to run his shop, if not only because routine was easier than confronting his grief. Boo mourned alongside him. In many ways the cat was all Sully had left of his wife.


One evening, desperate for distraction, Sully decided to try baking. The results were mixed at best. He baked several cakes of questionable appearance, though all were edible, and some were even a little tasty. Having baked far more than he and Boo could possibly consume, he set the cakes out in his shop for customers to enjoy while waiting for fittings or alterations. What happened next changed his life. For the first time in years, Sully saw genuine joy. The children of Vallaki lit up at the sight of the free treats. The children’s excitement touched Sully in a way he hadn’t expected and awoke something in him that he had believed long dead.


Word spread quickly. The children soon learned that old Sully was different from most adults in Barovia. He cared little for wealth, status, or family name. In his eyes, every child deserved kindness, regardless of their circumstances. The only things he asked for were good manners and respect for the clothes he made. Any child who could manage those two things would be welcomed with a warm smile, a kind word, a slice of cake, and perhaps a gentle pat on the head. For the first time since Edith's passing, Sully felt he had found something worth living for.


Then tragedy struck again. One afternoon, Boo crossed paths with the hunting hounds of a local noble. By the time Sully found him, it was too late. The loss plunged him into a grief even deeper than before. Edith had been gone for years, but Boo had remained as the last living connection to the life they had shared. Without Boo, Sully felt truly alone. Weeks passed in a haze. Then one evening when the mists happened to be quite thicker than normal, Sully spotted a familiar shape darting through a narrow alleyway. It was a white cat identical to Boo in every way.


Sully hurried after it with his heart racing. The cat led him deep into the twisting streets before finally stopping. As he approached, Sully saw that the cat was wounded. Without hesitating he scooped the animal into his arms and carried it home. He dressed its wounds and cared for it throughout the night. The next morning Sully woke to find the cat upright next to him staring as if it’d been waiting. Then it vanished in a puff of smoke. 

Standing where the cat had been was a genie. The mysterious being explained that Sully's compassion had not gone unnoticed. Even after suffering years of loss and heartbreak, he had chosen kindness over bitterness. Rather than turning away from suffering, he continued to help those in need. As a reward, the genie offered Sully a gift. It bestowed upon Sully a fragment of its power and taught him the secrets of magic. Most precious of all, it granted him a spell through which he could summon a faithful familiar, a white cat who looked, behaved, and carried the spirit of Boo.
The genie then turned its attention to an old silver pocket watch hanging from a chain around Sully's neck. The watch had been his most treasured possession for many years. Inside were two tiny portraits. One was of Edith, smiling as she had before illness stole her strength, and the other of Boo, curled up in his favorite sleeping position in the shop’s window sill. Sully carried the watch everywhere he went, often opening it during quiet moments to remember the family he had lost. The genie outstretched its hand and an arcane energy flowed into the watch. Bright runes briefly shimmered across its silver surface before fading from sight. The genie explained that the watch had become bound to the magic it had bestowed upon Sully and would serve as a tie to Sully’s new gifts.
From that day forward every spell Sully cast passed through the memories of those he loved most. When he summoned his familiar the watch would softly tick a few seconds backward before a puff of smoke appeared and Boo emerged once more at his side. Whenever he used  healing magic, the silver casing grew warm in his hand, as though Edith herself were encouraging him to continue caring for others just as he had cared for her.
Though Sully knew the familiar was not truly his lost companion, seeing those bright eyes again reminded him of something he had nearly forgotten. Love may be lost but kindness endures and as long as kindness remains in the world, so too does hope.
